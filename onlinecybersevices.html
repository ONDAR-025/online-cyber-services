COMPREHENSIVE GUIDE TO KEY GOVERNMENT E-SERVICES

HELB: Loan Application

The Higher Education Loans Board (HELB) provides student loans for Kenyan students in universities and technical institutions. The application is categorized as First-Time and Subsequent. Both require accurate data entry and document submission to avoid delays or rejection.

KRA: New PIN Registration

Kenya Revenue Authority (KRA) issues Personal Identification Numbers (PINs) that are essential for tax compliance. The registration is done online through the iTax system and is required for employment, opening bank accounts, or transacting with government entities.

KRA: Tax Returns Filing

All taxpayers in Kenya must file annual tax returns to remain compliant with the law. Returns are filed using the KRA iTax platform, and it is mandatory for both individuals and businesses whether or not they have income to declare.

KRA: Tax Compliance Certificate (TCC)

A Tax Compliance Certificate is issued by KRA as proof that a taxpayer's tax matters are up-to-date. This certificate is often required for job applications, tenders, and other formal engagements with public institutions.

KUCCPS: Student Portal Account Creation

The Kenya Universities and Colleges Central Placement Service (KUCCPS) enables students to create accounts and apply for tertiary institution placement. Account creation is the first step for course selection and is done using KCSE credentials.

KUCCPS: Course Selection/Revision

Once registered, students can select or revise their courses based on cluster points and institution availability. This is done during the official placement period announced by KUCCPS.

KUCCPS: Admission Letters

After successful placement, KUCCPS uploads admission details on their portal or the respective institution's website. Admission letters contain reporting dates, required documents, and fee structures.

HELB: Loan Repayment Statement & Compliance Certificate

HELB beneficiaries can access their repayment statements and request compliance certificates through the HELB portal. These documents are vital for tracking loan status and fulfilling employment or application requirements.

eCitizen: General Services

eCitizen is Kenya’s official digital platform for accessing various government services. Users can register and access services such as Police Clearance, Business Registration, Marriage, and Civil Registration Certificates.

NTSA: Motor & Driving Services

The National Transport and Safety Authority (NTSA) offers motor vehicle and driver licensing services online via the TIMS portal, including PSV License, Vehicle Inspection, and Smart DL applications.

NSSF: Member Services

The National Social Security Fund (NSSF) provides social security protection for Kenyan workers. Services include member registration and contribution status inquiries.

eNFS & Passport Services

Kenyans can apply for passports, visas, and alien cards through the eNFS platform linked via eCitizen. All applications require document verification and biometric capture.

Business & Company Services

Kenya’s Business Registration Service handles incorporation and compliance requests. Business names (BN) and company registrations (CR) are processed via eCitizen.

TSC: Teacher Registration & TPAD Portal

The Teachers Service Commission (TSC) handles professional registration and performance management through its TPAD portal. Registration is mandatory for practicing teachers.

DCI: Certificate of Good Conduct

The Certificate of Good Conduct, issued by the Directorate of Criminal Investigations (DCI), is a requirement for employment and visa applications. Applications are processed via eCitizen under the DCI portal.
